  • Conduct Market Research

    Who and where are your customers and competitors? How will you specialize and gain an advantage? To learn more:

    • Read about getting started in Florida
    • Read the U.S. Small Business Administration’s Business Guide
    • “SizeUp” your competition
    • Learn from resources shared by UF’s Warrington College of Business
    • Review local data with the Gainesville Open Data Portal

  • Write Your Business Plan

    Develop a written plan, a tool for summarizing and communicating your vision, objectives and strategies to succeed. Helpful for guiding your next steps in launching your dream, a well-crafted business plan can also facilitate support and investment in your idea. Some important content to craft is the:

    • Executive Summary

      Provide the most critical information first. Readers should immediately gain an understanding of what your business is and why it will be successful.

    • Introduction

      Provide the most critical information first. Readers should immediately gain an understanding of what your business is and why it will be successful.

    • Business Description

      Describe your product. Discuss its uniqueness and value to the community.

    • Market Analysis

      Share your research. Present the data and your conclusions about the forces of supply and demand you will encounter.

    • Organization and Management

      Introduce your team. Define their responsibilities. Fully outline your organization’s current and future structure. Begin with future growth in mind.

    • Marketing and Sales

      Plan your marketing campaign. Lay out your strategy to persuade consumers to purchase your product.

    • Financial Plan and Projections

      Show where the funds go. State your current financial status. Project future revenues. Use balance sheets and cash flow statements.

    • Supporting Documents

      Include additional documents and information that would be helpful in telling your story and soliciting support.

    Utilize the U.S. Small Business Administration’s business plan template as a guide to get started.
