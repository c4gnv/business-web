LEGALIZE

Preparing for launch requires formalizing your ideas into structure. Click below for local, state and federal resources to legalize and start doing business.

  • Identify Your Business Structure

    Decide on a structure that meets your individual needs and objectives.

    Here is a helpful resource provided by the SBA for comparing common structures:

    Business structure Ownership Liability Taxes
    Sole proprietorship One person Unlimited personal liability
    • Personal tax only
    Partnerships One person Unlimited personal liability
    • Self-employment tax (except for limited partners)
    • Personal tax
    Limited liability company (LLC) One or more people Owners are not personally liable
    • Self-employment tax
    • Personal tax or corporate tax
    Corporation - C corp One or more people Owners are not personally liable
    • Corporate tax
    Corporation - S corp One or more people, but no more than 100, and all must be U.S. citizens Owners are not personally liable
    • Personal tax
    Corporation - B corp One or more people Owners are not personally liable
    • Corporate tax
    Corporation - Nonprofit One or more people Owners are not personally liable
    • Tax-exempt, but corporate profits can't be distributed

  • Form Your Business

    • Form a Florida Profit or Non-Profit Corporation
      • Profit Articles of Incorporation
      • Non-Profit Articles of Incorporation
    • Form a Florida Limited Liability Company
      • LLC Articles of Organization
    • Form a Partnership
      • General Partnership, Florida Limited Partnership, or Foreign Limited Partnership

  • Register Your Business

    • In selecting your business name, you may need a trademark or service mark (http://dos.myflorida.com/sunbiz/forms/trademark-and-service-mark/), or a fictitious name (also known as a “doing business as” or “dba”) (http://dos.myflorida.com/sunbiz/start-business/efile/fl-fictitious-name-registration/).

    • A Fictitious Name Registration (https://efile.sunbiz.org/ficregintro.html) in Florida is not required for an individual’s legal name, or for the name of a corporation, limited liability company or limited partnership.

    • Obtain your business license from the City of Gainesville (https://eservices1.cityofgainesville.org/BusinessTax/6/Start.aspx).

    • Apply for an federal Employer Identification Number (EIN) with the IRS (https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/employer-id-numbers).

    • Many businesses in Florida are required to register with the Department of Business & Professional Regulation (http://www.myfloridalicense.com/dbpr/services.html).

    • Workers' Compensation insurance is mandatory for most employers in Florida. Learn more about the requirements (http://www.myfloridacfo.com/division/wc/Employer/default.htm#.WdU6h49SyCg) depending on your unique business.

    • You may be required to register (http://floridarevenue.com/dor/taxes/registration.html) to collect, accrue or send taxes or fees in Florida.
